About Us

Gaurav Gupta: Director of Education in the School of Computing at Macquarie University. He holds a Master of Computing from the National University of Singapore (2004) and a PhD in Information Security from Macquarie University (2008). With 20 years of experience teaching Mathematics and Computer Science at the university level in Australia, Gaurav strongly believes in the importance of strong fundamentals and first principles for long-term success and enjoyment of the subject.

Gunjan Chamania: Lecturer at Macquarie University. She holds a Master of Computing from the National University of Singapore (2004) and worked as a Salesforce Administrator for 10 years before making a career change into tertiary teaching in 2020. At G2 Tutoring, we provide personalised tutoring for students in Years 5–10. Our goal is to get children excited about learning, with academic success as a natural by-product.

Subjects We Cover

Mathematics
  • Number
    • Numeracy skills (fractions, decimals, percentages, integers)
    • Arithmetic operations
  • Algebra
    • Patterns and expressions
    • Linear equations and graphs
    • Introduction to quadratic equations (Years 9–10)
  • Measurement
    • Length, area, volume
    • Units and conversions
    • Time and rates
  • Space (Geometry)
    • Shapes and angles
    • Congruence and similarity
    • Pythagoras’ theorem
    • Trigonometry (basic right-angled)
  • Statistics
    • Data displays and interpretation
    • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode, range)
    • Scatterplots and correlation
  • Probability
    • Chance experiments
    • Independent and dependent events
    • Theoretical vs experimental probability
Computer Programming
  • Python: Variables, conditions, loops, functions, lists, dictionaries; classes introduced in upper years.
  • Java: Variables, operators, loops, arrays, ArrayLists, basic object-oriented programming.
  • Processing (visual coding): Animation and visual programming; suitable for Years 5–7.
  • HTML + CSS: Basic web development; creating simple webpages.
